Complete Tool Guide

Everything you need to know about each of PixelForge's 29 free image tools.

🗜️ Image Compressor

The Image Compressor tool reduces the file size of your photos without a visible drop in quality. It uses a smart lossy compression algorithm that can shrink JPEG and PNG files by 50–85% on average. This is perfect for speeding up websites, reducing email attachment sizes, and saving storage space. You can fine-tune the quality level with a simple slider, and the tool shows you a live before/after file size comparison so you know exactly what you're getting.

📐 Image Resizer

The Image Resizer lets you change the dimensions of any image by entering a specific width and height, or by choosing from popular presets like 1080×1080 for Instagram posts, 1920×1080 for HD wallpapers, and many more. You can choose to maintain the original aspect ratio to prevent your image from looking stretched or distorted. This tool is ideal for preparing images for social media platforms, website uploads, and print materials.

🔄 Format Converter

The Format Converter allows you to switch your image between all major formats including JPG, PNG, WebP, AVIF, BMP, and GIF. Converting to WebP can reduce file sizes by 30–40% compared to JPEG while maintaining the same visual quality — making it the preferred format for modern web development. Converting to PNG is ideal when you need a lossless format with transparency support for logos, icons, and graphic design work.

✨ HD Enhancer

The HD Enhancer tool upscales your images and improves their visual clarity using advanced sharpening and detail recovery algorithms. It's particularly useful for restoring old or low-resolution photos, enlarging product images for e-commerce listings, and improving the quality of compressed screenshots. The tool applies multi-pass sharpening and contrast enhancement to produce crisper, more detailed output images.

✂️ Image Cropper

The Image Cropper lets you select any rectangular area of your image to keep, and discards the rest. Simply drag to draw a selection box over the area you want, and the coordinates are shown in real time so you can be precise. Cropping is essential for removing unwanted backgrounds, focusing attention on the subject, and preparing images for platforms that require specific aspect ratios like profile pictures and cover photos.

📄 Image to PDF

The Image to PDF tool converts one or multiple images into a single PDF document. You can choose between A4, Letter, and custom page sizes, and control whether the image fills the full page or is placed with margins. This tool is extremely useful for scanning documents with your phone and sharing them as professional PDF files, combining product photos into a catalog, or submitting photos as part of official documents.

💧 Watermark Tool

The Watermark tool adds text or a semi-transparent overlay to your image to protect your intellectual property or brand your content. You can customize the text, font size, color, opacity, and position. Photographers use this to stamp their name or website on images before sharing them online. Businesses use it to add copyright notices, "CONFIDENTIAL" labels, or brand logos to official documents and promotional photos.

🎨 Photo Adjustments

The Photo Adjustments tool gives you control over brightness, contrast, saturation, hue, and sharpness using intuitive sliders. Web Performance

How to Optimize Images for Faster Websites

Images are typically the largest assets on any webpage, often accounting for 60–80% of total page weight. Unoptimized images slow down your site, increase bounce rates, and hurt your Google search ranking. Here's how to fix that with PixelForge.

Step 1: Choose the right format. For photographs, use JPEG or WebP. PNG is best reserved for images that need transparency (logos, icons). WebP offers 30–40% better compression than JPEG at the same quality level, and is now supported by all modern browsers. Use PixelForge's Format Converter to batch-convert your JPEGs to WebP.

Step 2: Resize before compressing. Never upload a 4000px-wide photo when your website only displays it at 800px wide. The browser has to download all those extra pixels and then discard them. Use the Image Resizer to reduce dimensions first, then use the Compressor to fine-tune file size.

Step 3: Aim for 80–90% quality. Most people cannot tell the difference between a JPEG at 100% quality and one at 85% quality, but the file size difference can be enormous. A quality setting of 82–88% is generally the sweet spot — significant size reduction with imperceptible quality loss.

Step 4: Strip EXIF data. Your camera embeds metadata into every photo — including GPS coordinates, camera model, and software details. This adds unnecessary kilobytes. Use PixelForge's Remove EXIF tool to clean all metadata before uploading to your site.

Photography Tips

Protecting Your Photos Online: A Practical Guide

Once a photo is published online, it can easily be downloaded, shared, and used without your permission. While there's no foolproof way to stop all unauthorized use, there are several practical steps you can take to protect your work and make it harder for others to use without credit.

Add a visible watermark. A subtle watermark with your name, logo, or website URL makes your authorship clear and discourages casual theft. Use PixelForge's Watermark tool to add semi-transparent text to the corner of your images. Choose an opacity of 30–50% so it's visible but doesn't distract from the image itself.

Remove GPS metadata before sharing. Every smartphone photo contains EXIF data with your precise GPS coordinates embedded in the file. Sharing these images online reveals exactly where the photo was taken — including your home address if you photograph at home. Use the Remove EXIF tool to strip location data before uploading.

Publish at web resolution, not full size. Resize your images to the maximum display size needed (usually 1200–1920px wide) before publishing. This deters people who want to print or resell your high-resolution original files, since they only have access to the web-sized version.

Compress strategically. Compressed images are harder to use commercially since the quality loss becomes visible when printed large. You can keep the quality high enough for web display while making the file less suitable for print reproduction.

Social Media

The Complete Image Size Guide for Social Media in 2026

Each social media platform has its own recommended image dimensions, and uploading the wrong size leads to blurry crops, awkward white bars, and unprofessional-looking posts. Here's a quick reference guide to the most important sizes, and how to prepare your images correctly using PixelForge.

Instagram: Square posts work best at 1080×1080px. Portrait posts (which get more screen space in the feed) should be 1080×1350px. Stories and Reels use a 1080×1920px vertical format. Profile pictures are displayed as circles at 110px but should be uploaded at 320×320px for sharpness.

LinkedIn: Regular posts display best at 1200×627px. Company logos should be 300×300px. Profile photos should be uploaded at 400×400px or larger. Cover photos are displayed at 1128×191px on desktop.

Facebook: Link preview images should be 1200×630px. Event covers display at 1920×1080px. Profile photos should be at least 320×320px and are displayed as circles.

How to resize quickly: Use PixelForge's Image Resizer and select the platform preset from the dropdown — all the common sizes are pre-programmed so you just choose the platform and click process. For profile photos on any platform, use the Circle Crop tool to get a perfectly round result.
